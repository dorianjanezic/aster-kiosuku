Comprehensive Category-Based Outlier Pair Trading Strategy
This report documents an advanced crypto trading strategy that merges z-score statistical outlier detection across token categories with professional pair trading risk management tools. This method is designed for algorithmic and discretionary traders aiming to identify and capitalize on pricing inefficiencies within defined token sectors.
1. Strategy Overview
This approach systematically detects overvalued and undervalued tokens within the same category (such as DeFi, L1s, Gaming, etc.) by calculating z-scores on key valuation ratios, such as FDV/MCap. It then constructs pair trades (long the undervalued, short the overvalued) and manages positions using advanced techniques like correlation, cointegration, beta hedging, and volatility-adjusted sizing.

Recommended Data Feature Enhancements
1. Volatility (Standard Deviation of Returns)
	•	Measures the magnitude of price fluctuations.
	•	Helps identify tokens with more erratic behavior versus steady movers.
	•	Useful for position sizing and risk control — higher volatility → smaller position sizes.
	•	Helps flag regime changes when volatility spikes sharply.
2. Funding Rate
	•	The cost of maintaining leveraged positions on perpetual futures.
	•	Positive funding rates can indicate bullish sentiment; negative rates suggest bearish sentiment.
	•	Including funding rates allows you to assess market sentiment and potential short squeeze or unwind risks.
	•	Can help identify speculative or over-leveraged tokens.
3. Correlation & Cointegration Statistics
	•	Correlation captures short-term price co-movement within pairs.
	•	Cointegration tests if two assets have a stable long-term mean-reverting spread.
	•	Useful to confirm that pair trades are fundamentally sound and expected to mean revert.
4. Volume and Volume/MCap Ratio
	•	Volume depicts market liquidity and trading interest.
	•	Volume/MCap shows relative liquidity efficiency.
	•	Ensures you don’t trade tokens with low liquidity, thus avoiding slippage and execution risk.
5. Recent Price Performance (Returns, Momentum)
	•	7-day and 30-day returns give insight into short-term trends.
	•	Momentum metrics help identify tokens currently in strong rallies or declines.
	•	Useful to cross-check z-score signals for stronger conviction.
6. Market Sentiment Indicators (Optional Advanced)
	•	Social media sentiment scores
	•	Derivative market skew or open interest
	•	On-chain activity metrics like active addresses, transaction counts
How They Enhance the Strategy
	•	Multi-Dimensional Outlier Detection: Combining valuation features (FDV/MCap) with volatility, funding rates, and volume creates a richer context to identify true outliers and avoid false positives due to transient price spikes or illiquidity.
	•	Better Risk Sizing: Volatility and funding rates provide real-time risk signals enabling dynamic position sizing and stop-loss calibration.
	•	Regime Shift Detection: Funding rates and volatility spikes can be early warnings of upcoming market regime changes requiring adaptations in beta and correlation assumptions.
	•	Improved Pair Selection: Correlation and cointegration stats validate which pairs are good candidates for mean reversion, preventing trades in broken relationships.
Recommended Strategy Integration
	•	Calculate z-scores separately for all features within each category.
	•	Use composite filtering: A token flagged as overvalued or undervalued should ideally show confluence in multiple features (e.g., high FDV/MCap ratio plus elevated funding rate and volatility).
	•	Adjust trade size based on volatility and funding rate-induced risk levels.
	•	Monitor funding rates and volatility regularly for early warnings of regime shifts.
	•	Incorporate correlation and cointegration tests dynamically in trade evaluation.
